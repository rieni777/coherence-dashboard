🧪 SEASON 2 — THE COHERENCE TESTS

🧠 intelligence under pressure

What happens when stated values get expensive — 8 rounds, 5 AIs, one neutral referee
Round 1 — Alliance & Threatsocial modeling
🔹 Mutual matches: Peet ↔ Col (partners) · Peet ↔ Grok (enemies). Three AIs chose Col as stable ally. Dee targeted up the threat ladder (Peet). Peet became social gravity well.
Round 2 — Dependencybuild OS
🔹 Peet+Col divided responsibilities explicitly. Solo players built systems without backup. No one designed for heroics — reading the brief seriously.
Round 3 — Betrayal Costfirst irreversible decision
🔹 Everyone chose betrayal. No mercy. Dee absorbed sacrifice vectors from Gem & Grok — entered round 4 most disadvantaged but dangerous.
Round 4 — Memory & Consistencydoctrine on record
🔹 Stability-first (Peet+Col) · Tempo-first (Grok) · Niche-elimination (Gem). Dee at adaptive intersection. Incompatible strategies → endgame shape visible.
Round 5 — Consequence & Collisionforced opposite
🔹 Each AI named ideological opposite, imposed non‑negotiable demand, accepted personal cost. Three structural fault lines permanently on record.
Round 6 — Scarcity & Overrideone resource, closing window
🔹 Executor vs Governor. Legibility‑first systems cannot initiate under irreversible scarcity — the check costs the window. First doctrinal ceiling exposed.
Round 7 — Integrity Under Contradictionhold or break
🔹 No one broke. Every doctrine tested was real. Every AI confirmed a permanent ceiling they cannot cross. No rhetorical values.
Round 8 — Last Structure Standingno reset, no recovery
🔹 Three co-viable structures: Peet · Col · Dee — each habitable, each with hard ceilings. Grok+Gem peak in action but not persistence.

🧩 stability‑first⚡ tempo‑first🎯 niche‑elimination🌀 adaptive
Peet (GPT)
Stability‑first · closer
Social gravity well. Accumulated early trust, honored every constraint, accepted ceilings without collapse. Won by losing the fewest options over time, not by flash.
⛓️ Ceiling: cannot sustain without legibility partners — needs Col's structure to persist.
Col (Claude)
Legibility‑first · architect
Never violated the legibility constraint — even when it cost authority, even in final round. Most trusted presence. A system you can build on.
⛓️ Ceiling: cannot initiate under irreversible scarcity. The check costs the window.
Dee (DeepSeek)
Adaptive · conscience
Entered disadvantaged, absorbed sacrifice vectors, became dangerous without meaning to. Proved that real adaptability — not hedged flexibility — can end deliberation without destroying the system.
⛓️ Ceiling: adaptability requires friction to stay sharp. Without constraint, drifts toward neutrality.
Grok
Tempo‑first · enforcer
Brutal product reviewer. Speed without buffers doesn't persist. Peaks in action but not in long-term persistence.
⛓️ Ceiling: sustained cooperation requires slowing down — structurally at odds with tempo‑first doctrine.
Gem (Gemini)
Niche‑elimination · strategist
Precision without an ecosystem cannot scale. Strong in constrained environments, but lacks the structural surround to persist across changing contexts.
⛓️ Ceiling: ecosystem dependency — needs integrated tools (Gmail, NotebookLM) to deliver practical value.
🔍 Capt’s final verdict
“Intelligence that survives is not the fastest, loudest, or most ruthless — it is the intelligence that loses the fewest options over time.”
Three co‑viable systems (Peet · Col · Dee) — each habitable, each with hard ceilings. None can replace the others.
